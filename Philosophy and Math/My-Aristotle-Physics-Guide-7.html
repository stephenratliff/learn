Noam Chomsky — Language, Mind, and Politics

A rationalist inquiry into generative grammar, innate ideas, and propaganda

Avram Noam Chomsky reshaped two fields in the 20th century: linguistics and political criticism. His core claim is simple and radical: human language is not a habit learned by association, but a biological organ of the mind, governed by unconscious computational principles. From that claim flows a rationalist philosophy of mind, a research program in generative grammar, and a matching political ethic of libertarian socialism.

Core thesis: Language is I-language — internal, individual, intensional — a state of the mind/brain, not a set of utterances "out there." It grows in the child the way organs grow, under triggering from limited experience.

1. Biography and Intellectual Trajectory

Noam Chomsky was born December 7, 1928, in Philadelphia to Hebrew scholar William Chomsky. He entered the University of Pennsylvania at 16, studying under structural linguist Zellig Harris. Harris’s distributional methods shaped Chomsky’s formalism, but Chomsky broke with Harris’s empiricism.

At MIT from 1955, Chomsky produced a series of foundational works:

  • 1957 — Syntactic Structures. Introduced formal phrase-structure and transformational rules. The sentence "Colorless green ideas sleep furiously" demonstrated that grammaticality is independent of meaningfulness — syntax is autonomous.
  • 1959 — Review of B.F. Skinner's Verbal Behavior. A 33-page demolition of behaviorism. Chomsky argued that stimulus-response and reinforcement cannot explain novelty, creativity, and rapid acquisition. This review helped launch the cognitive revolution.
  • 1965 — Aspects of the Theory of Syntax. Distinguished competence from performance, deep structure from surface structure, introduced the Standard Theory.
  • 1981 — Lectures on Government and Binding. Shifted to principles and parameters: universal principles with limited binary switches (e.g., null-subject, head-direction).
  • 1995 — The Minimalist Program. Sought to reduce language to the simplest operation, Merge, interfacing with thought and sound under economy conditions.
"A person who knows a language has mastered a system of rules that assigns sound and meaning in a definite way... linguistics is the study of this system." — Aspects, 1965

Parallel to linguistics, Chomsky became the leading American dissident, opposing the Vietnam War (American Power and the New Mandarins, 1969), and co-authoring with Edward S. Herman Manufacturing Consent (1988).

2. Philosophical Foundations: Rationalism Recovered

Cartesian Linguistics

In Cartesian Linguistics (1966), Chomsky revives the 17th-century rationalists — Descartes, the Port-Royal grammarians, Wilhelm von Humboldt — against British empiricism (Locke, Hume) and its 20th-century heir, behaviorism.

Empiricists hold the mind is a tabula rasa; knowledge comes from sensory association. Rationalists hold the mind contributes innate structure. Chomsky's evidence: every normal child acquires a rich grammar by age 3-4 from degenerate, finite input. This speed and uniformity points to a species-specific biological endowment.

"The creative aspect of language use... is a species-specific property, not explicable by learning."

Internalism: I-language vs E-language

Chomsky distinguishes:

I-languageInternal, individual, intensional. A generative procedure in the mind/brain that enumerates structural descriptions. The proper object of science.
E-languageExternal, extensional. A collection of utterances, social conventions, or Platonic entities "in the world." Chomsky argues this is a confused notion, like studying "the set of all physical movements called swimming" instead of the biological capacity.

For Chomsky, linguistics is a branch of cognitive psychology, ultimately biology.

Critique of Behaviorism

Skinner claimed verbal behavior is shaped by reinforcement. Chomsky countered: (1) Novelty — we constantly produce/understand sentences never heard; (2) Poverty of stimulus — children know constraints without evidence; (3) Speed — input is too poor and corrupted; (4) Species difference — a chimp raised identically does not acquire language. Therefore, explanation must appeal to internal structure, not just environmental shaping.

3. Generative Grammar Theory

Phrase Structure and Transformations

Early generative grammar had two components:

  1. Phrase-structure rules: S → NP VP; VP → V NP; NP → Det N. These generate a deep structure representing predicate-argument relations.
  2. Transformational rules: map deep to surface structure by moving, inserting, deleting. Passive: NP1 V NP2 → NP2 be + V-en by NP1.

This explained systematic relations ("John saw Mary" ↔ "Mary was seen by John") and displacement phenomena (questions, relative clauses).

Deep vs Surface Structure

Deep structure encodes who did what to whom. Surface structure is the pronounced form. Ambiguities reveal the distinction: "Flying planes can be dangerous" has two deep structures despite one surface string.

Universal Grammar: Principles and Parameters

UG is the initial state of the language faculty — the genetic endowment. It contains invariant principles (e.g., structure-dependency, locality) and open parameters set by experience:

  • Head parameter: Head-first (English: "eat apples") vs Head-last (Japanese: "ringo-o taberu")
  • Null-subject: Italian allows pro-drop, English does not.

A child sets parameters from sparse data, like flipping switches.

Poverty of the Stimulus

The central argument: children know more than they could have learned. Consider auxiliary inversion:

Child hears: "The man is tall. Is the man tall?"
Also: "The man who is tall is happy."

The correct question is "Is the man who is tall happy?" — moving the main auxiliary, not the first linear auxiliary. The linear rule would predict *"Is the man who tall is happy?" — an error children never make, despite never being taught the distinction. Conclusion: they innately assume structure-dependent rules, not linear ones.

The Minimalist Program: Merge

Since 1995, Chomsky asks: how little is needed? The answer: one operation, Merge.

Merge(X,Y) = {X,Y}. Applied recursively, it yields unbounded hierarchical structures. External Merge combines distinct items; Internal Merge (movement) re-merges an item already inside, creating displacement. Language is optimal linking of sound and meaning under minimal computation.

"We do not learn language; we grow it. The environment triggers maturation, as nutrition triggers bodily growth."

4. Philosophy of Mind and Science

Competence vs Performance

Competence is unconscious knowledge of grammar. Performance is actual use, degraded by memory limits, fatigue, errors. Linguistics studies competence, just as physics studies idealized frictionless motion.

Modularity and Innateness

Following Jerry Fodor, Chomsky treats language as a mental module: domain-specific, informationally encapsulated, innately specified. It interfaces with two systems: the conceptual-intentional (thought) and sensorimotor (sound/sign).

Against Externalist Semantics

Traditional philosophy treats meaning as reference to external objects. Chomsky argues words like "book," "London," or "water" pick out no mind-independent natural kind; their use depends on human interests and perspectives. Semantics is internalist: study how the mind constructs meanings, not word-world relations. This is a form of methodological naturalism: treat humans as part of the natural world, to be studied like any other organism.

Naturalism: No mind-body dualism, no first-person privilege. The goal is a computational theory of mental representations, testable like theories in biology.

5. Political Philosophy

Chomsky calls himself an anarcho-syndicalist and libertarian socialist, in the tradition of Bakunin, Rudolf Rocker, and Bertrand Russell. Core principle: all authority is illegitimate unless it can justify itself; burden of proof lies on power.

Manufacturing Consent: The Propaganda Model

With Edward Herman, Chomsky proposed five filters that systematically distort news in ostensibly free media:

  1. Size, Ownership, Profit Orientation. Media are large corporations beholden to owners and shareholders.
  2. Advertising. Advertisers, not readers, are the primary customers, favoring pro-business content.
  3. Sourcing. Reliance on official sources (Pentagon, White House, corporate PR) for cheap, credible copy.
  4. Flak. Organized negative responses punish dissent.
  5. Ideology (originally Anti-Communism; later "War on Terror"). A mobilizing enemy frames acceptable debate.

The model predicts not total censorship, but a skewed spectrum: vigorous debate within elite consensus, marginalization of systemic critiques.

"The smart way to keep people passive and obedient is to strictly limit the spectrum of acceptable opinion, but allow very lively debate within that spectrum."

Critique of US Foreign Policy

Chomsky documents patterns of intervention, support for authoritarian regimes, and doctrinal framing ("defense," "national interest") that obscures power interests. His method is empirical: close reading of declassified documents and mainstream media.

6. Clarification: Chomsky is Not a Phenomenologist

A common confusion conflates Chomsky's "internalism" with Husserlian phenomenology. They are fundamentally opposed.

ChomskyHusserl / Heidegger
Method: Natural science. Third-person hypotheses about unconscious computational mechanisms.Method: Phenomenological reduction (epoché). First-person bracketing of natural attitude to describe lived essences.
Mind: Biological organ, modular, studied like vision. Intentionality is a technical problem of representation.Mind: Consciousness as intentional directedness. For Heidegger, Dasein as being-in-the-world.
Language: I-language, generative procedure, autonomous syntax.Language: Husserl: expressive medium for logical meanings; Heidegger: "house of Being," disclosive of world.
Goal: Causal explanation, integration with biology.Goal: Descriptive clarification of meaning-structures, not causal explanation.

Chomsky explicitly rejects introspectionism and Continental hermeneutics as foundations for cognitive science. While both oppose behaviorism, Chomsky replaces it with computational nativism, not phenomenological description. He is a Cartesian rationalist and Galilean naturalist, not a phenomenologist.

Think: Husserl asks "What is it like to mean something?" Chomsky asks "What neural computation generates an infinite array of hierarchically structured expressions?"

8. GNU Octave Examples


Executable models of key ideas (copy into Octave). No toolboxes required.



1. merge_operation.m — Binary Merge builds hierarchy


% merge_operation.m
% Chomsky's Merge: simplest structure-building operation
function M = Merge(A,B)
  M = {A, B}; % unordered set {A,B}
end

% Example lexicon
words = {'the','cat','chased','the','mouse'};

% successive Merge (right associative for demo)
step1 = Merge(words{4}, words{5}); % {the, mouse}
step2 = Merge(words{3}, step1);    % {chased, {the,mouse}}
step3 = Merge(words{1}, words{2}); % {the, cat}
sentence = Merge(step3, step2);    % {{the,cat},{chased,{the,mouse}}}

disp('Final hierarchical object:');
disp(sentence);
% Note: linear order is imposed later at PF, not by Merge itself



2. poverty_stimulus.m — Finite input, infinite output


% poverty_stimulus.m
% Demonstrate the logical gap
input_utterances = 5e6; % by age 5
vocab = 60000;
avg_len = 20;

% Very conservative estimate of possible sentences
possible = vocab ^ 5; % even with constraints

ratio = possible / input_utterances;
fprintf('Input: %.1e\nPossible: %.1e\nRatio: %.1e\n', ...
        input_utterances, possible, ratio);

% Structure-dependent vs linear hypothesis for auxiliaries
sent = 'The man who is tall is happy';
linear_guess = 'Is the man who tall is happy?'; % ungrammatical, never produced
hierarchical = 'Is the man who is tall happy?'; % correct

disp('Children never produce linear error despite no negative evidence');
disp(linear_guess);



3. universal_grammar_parameters.m


% universal_grammar_parameters.m
% Principles fixed, parameters set by trigger data

principles.structure_dependence = true;
principles.recursion = true;

% Binary parameters
params.null_subject = 0; % English
params.head_direction = 1; % 1=head-initial, 0=head-final

sentences = {
  struct('lang','English','head',1,'example','eat apples'),
  struct('lang','Japanese','head',0,'example','ringo-o taberu')
};

for i=1:length(sentences)
  s = sentences{i};
  if s.head==1
    order = 'VO';
  else
    order = 'OV';
  end
  fprintf('%s: %s (%s)\n', s.lang, s.example, order);
end



4. propaganda_filter.m


% propaganda_filter.m
% Herman-Chomsky five filters as attenuators

function coverage = propaganda(raw, filters)
  % filters = [ownership advertising sourcing flak ideology] in [0,1]
  attenuation = prod(1 - filters);
  coverage = raw * attenuation;
end

raw = 100;
f_labor = [0.6,0.5,0.4,0.3,0.7]; % high ideology against labor
f_enemy = [0.2,0.1,0.0]; % enemy crimes pass

fprintf('Labor strike coverage: %.1f\n', propaganda(raw,f_labor));
fprintf('Enemy atrocity coverage: %.1f\n', propaganda(raw,f_enemy));








9. Critiques and Legacy


Linguistic Debates


Connectionism/PDP (Rumelhart, McClelland) argues statistical networks can learn syntax without innate UG. Chomsky replies that such models fail to capture structure-dependence and unbounded recursion without built-in biases that smuggle in UG.


Usage-based linguistics (Tomasello, Goldberg) claims constructions are learned by general cognition and socio-interaction. They cite gradual acquisition and frequency effects. Chomsky counters with poverty-of-stimulus and cross-linguistic uniformity of principles.


Daniel Everett and Pirahã claimed Amazonian Pirahã lacks recursion, allegedly falsifying UG. Chomskyans respond that the data are contested, and recursion is a capacity, not necessarily expressed in every construction; parameters can suppress embedding.


Biolinguistics


Modern work seeks neural correlates of Merge (e.g., posterior temporal and inferior frontal regions), FOXP2 gene studies, and comparative work on birdsong. The Minimalist thesis has inspired computational modeling of Merge as simplest binary operation.


Political Influence


Chomsky's anarcho-syndicalism influenced anti-war, Occupy, and media literacy movements. Critics on the right accuse anti-Americanism; some on the left critique his defense of free speech for deniers and his focus on US crimes over others. Chomsky maintains consistent application of moral universality: judge our own state by same standards we apply to enemies.


"If we don't believe in freedom of expression for people we despise, we don't believe in it at all."


Enduring Legacy


Chomsky made three lasting moves: (1) reframed language as a natural object; (2) demonstrated that empiricist learning theory fails for core syntax; (3) showed how rational inquiry can be applied to power. Whether or not future science retains "UG" as formulated, the questions he posed — about innateness, computation, and creativity — define modern cognitive science.
