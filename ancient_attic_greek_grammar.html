The nominative singular of 3rd-declension nouns is unpredictable; always learn the genitive to determine the stem. The dat. pl. Homeric forms in -εσσι are common; Attic prose prefers -σι.

The 2nd aorist uses secondary active endings on a different (often shorter) stem. The root aorist adds secondary endings directly to the bare root, with no thematic vowel (ο/ε).
