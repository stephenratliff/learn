Declensions · Conjugations · Enclitics  —  Stems & Endings Only

Endings added to the stem. Sg = singular; Pl = plural. Nom = Nominative, Gen = Genitive, Dat = Dative, Acc = Accusative, Abl = Ablative, Voc = Vocative, Loc = Locative.
A dash (—) in the Nominative means the stem itself is the form; "= Nom" means identical to Nominative.

Endings are appended directly to the verb stem. PPP = Perfect Passive Participle (declines 2-1-2). Active personal endings (pres. system): -ō/-m, -s, -t, -mus, -tis, -nt. Passive personal endings (pres. system): -r, -ris/-re, -tur, -mur, -minī, -ntur.

Enclitics are suffixes appended directly to the end of a word. They are written without a space and shift the accent to the syllable immediately before them. The four primary Latin enclitics are -que, -ve, -ne, and -cum.
