Hablo, hablas, habla…
Spanish grammar, color-keyed.

One rule holds across every chart on this page: each grammatical person owns one color, everywhere. Yo is always carmine, tú is always gold, nosotros is always cobalt. Verb endings are set in bold mono in the person's color; stems stay in ink. On the noun side, masculine is always blue, feminine always red. Learn the key once and every table below reads at a glance.

Persona key yo tú él · ella · Ud. nosotros/-as vosotros/-as ellos · ellas · Uds.

1Articles

Spanish nouns don't decline by case, but articles and adjectives agree in gender and number — the closest thing Spanish has to declension. The coding here: masculine = cobalt, feminine = carmine, neuter = olive.

Watch out: feminine nouns beginning with stressed a-/ha- take el/un in the singular but stay feminine: el agua fría → las aguas frías. Contractions are mandatory: a + el = al, de + el = del.

Le → se rule: when an indirect and direct object pronoun stack, le/les becomes se: Se lo di — "I gave it to him," never le lo di. Order is always indirect before direct: me lo, te la, se los…

Accent marks matter: hablo = "I speak" (present) but habló = "he spoke" (preterite). The written accent is the only difference.

10Present subjunctive

The "opposite vowel" tense: -ar verbs take e-endings, -er/-ir verbs take a-endings. Build it from the yo form of the present: drop -o, add the swapped endings.

hablar · comer · vivir-ar → e -er → a -ir → a
Personhablarcomervivir
yohablecomaviva
túhablescomasvivas
él / ella / Ud.hablecomaviva
nosotros/-ashablemoscomamosvivamos
vosotros/-ashabléiscomáisviváis
ellos / ellas / Uds.hablencomanvivan
The yo-form trick: because the base is the present yo form, irregular yo verbs carry their irregularity through: tengo → tenga, conozco → conozca, digo → diga. Six verbs ignore the rule entirely: sea, esté, vaya, haya, sepa, dé.

13Compound tenses — one formula

Every perfect tense is just haber (conjugated) + past participle. The participle never changes: -ar → -ado, -er/-ir → -ido.

Present perfect — "I have spoken"haber + participle
Personhaber+ participleEnglish
yohehabladoI have spoken

Irregular participles worth memorizing: abierto (abrir), dicho (decir), escrito (escribir), hecho (hacer), muerto (morir), puesto (poner), roto (romper), visto (ver), vuelto (volver).
Swap haber's tense, get a new tense: había hablado (pluperfect) · habré hablado (future perfect) · habría hablado (conditional perfect) · haya hablado (perfect subjunctive). One formula, five tenses.
